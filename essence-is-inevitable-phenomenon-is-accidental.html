Essence is Inevitable, Phenomenon is Accidental

On Inevitability and Accidentality

Many people said Shanghai has finally lifted its lockdown. This “finally” reveals infinite anticipation and helplessness, ultimately leading to a result.

But the fact is simply this: Shanghai lifting the lockdown was inevitable. June 1st was merely a matter of time; the past two months were just a process.

It’s like the universe inevitably moving towards disorder. This is inevitable.

For most people, they will experience birth, student years, adulthood, marriage, childbirth, death, and so on. These are inevitable processes. Occasionally, some cases are non-inevitable—unable to be born, unable to attend school, unable to marry or have children, and so on.

These are accidental for the majority but inevitable for the individual cases.

I have a very basic viewpoint: as long as you put your heart into something, there will basically be a result, whether the result is good or bad, it’s just a matter of how much of the expectation is met.

The process is a unidirectional, irreversible movement. Movement has a starting point and an ending point. This is inevitable. It’s just that when the starting point is and when the ending point is—this is non-inevitable.

Phenomena are accidental; essence is inevitable.

Most people are keen to immerse themselves in phenomena, even indulging in a single success or failure.

In fact, the past is inevitable; only the future is accidental.

Similarly, most people will definitely have friends, but whether those friends are you is accidental. Friendship and love are both precious because, in the vast sea of people, finding a confidant or encountering mutual understanding and love is accidental, a matter of probability, and something to be grateful for.

Reason is inevitable; emotion is accidental.

Accidentality is artistic and romantic; inevitability is dull and tasteless.
